TL;DR: Teaser in a nutshell

Please keep in mind: All things, especially numeric values, are subject to change since the update is still in development.

General info

  • 5 PvE arenas in which a team of up to 5 players can fight against waves of monsters. If you dare, you can also face the beasts alone.
  • You can choose between 5 difficulty levels, each of them corresponds to a certain level range. Difficulty 1: up to level 99. 2: up to level 199. 3: up to level 299. 4: up to level 399. 5: can be played by all level ranges.
  • Each difficulty level consists of 10 monster waves. Monsters vary in strength. Difficulty increases as waves progress.
  • Each wave consists of 8 monsters (except for wave 10: 9-10 monsters depending on difficulty level). As soon as the last monster of a wave has been slain, the next wave starts.
  • If you do not manage to kill all monsters of a wave within 60 seconds, the battle ends. The battle also ends if all members of your team are dead.
  • PvE arenas feature randomly placed dynamic traps, obstacles and explosive barrels. So while the arena map as such is static, traps and their location will differ from fight to fight.
  • The usual death penalty does not apply in arena fights. Monsters do not yield XP or carry loot.
  • Only premium players can fight in PvE arenas. However, free account players can watch fights from above similar to PvP arenas.

    Rewards

  • Your team will receive a score depending on your fight performance (difficulty level, wave completion speed, number of waves completed). The scoring formula will be provided on the test board during the test of the summer update.
  • There are ingame leaderboards that show the current top 5 of each difficulty level as well as your top 5 scores.
  • If a team completes all 10 waves of a battle, each team member who survived until the end will be rewarded with arena badges (number based on difficulty level). Those badges can be exchanged for potions at the arena reward NPC. With a bit of luck, you may receive a special surprise cube when exchanging these badges for potions.

    Set up and rules

  • PvE arenas can be accessed through teleports next to PvP arenas in Thais, Kazordoon, Edron, Ankrahmun, and Rathleton.
  • Once a fight has been set up successfully, all participants of the upcoming fight need to assemble in the anteroom. You will then be teleported into the arena and the first wave will begin after 10 seconds.
  • There is a cooldown of 15 minutes before you can take part in a new PvE arena fight.
  • There is an entrance fee of 1000 gold for each participant (withdrawn from bank account).
  • PvP features like friendly fire will match the PvP options of your game world type.

    Website

  • A leaderboards section will be added to the website. There, you can find your game world's top scorers of the currently running period. Filters allow you to check the scores for each difficulty level and team size.
  • A period starts at the first day of a month and runs until the first day of the next month. Then, the final result of this period will be archived and a new period starts (all scores set to 0 again). Besides the currently running period, you can also view leaderboards of previous months on the website.
  • While ingame leaderboards tell you the current top scores right at the moment, leaderboards on the website are updated once per day.
