Further bits and pieces about imbuing...

General remark:

Please note that all contents of the teaser and this post, especially numeric values, are subject to change . The private test of the winter update is around the corner and we are looking forward to tweak and optimise imbuing with the help of your feedback. The initial values we have set are a starting point for the private test and we are open to constructive suggestions on how to improve them.

  • To be able to use imbuing shrines you need to collect a certain number of heavy old tomes for Albinius (Astral Shaper temple near Thais).
  • Gold fees to carry out an imbuement: basic = 5,000 gold; intricate = 25,000 gold; powerful = 100,000 gold.
  • Success rate if you carry out an imbuement: basic = 90%; intricate = 70%; powerful = 50%. Imbuing an item will always consume the required astral sources and gold fee, no matter if the imbuement succeeds or not.
  • Once an item has been imbued successfully, the effect lasts for 20 hours.
  • Number of imbuement slots on imbuable items:

    • 1-handed weapons, wands, rods: 2 slots
    • 2-handed weapons, bows: 3 slots
    • shields, spellbooks: 1 slot
    • helmets: up to 2 slots
    • armor (torso): up to 2 slots
    • boots: 1 slot



  • Items with active imbuements cannot be traded via the Market but you can trade them with other players via safe trade, for example.
  • As long as a certain imbuement effect is still active (=still has time remaining) on a particular item, you can neither imbue this item with the same imbuement (e.g. fire protection + 15%) again nor can you carry out an imbuement on this very item that grants the same type of effect (e.g. fire protection + 9%). In general, imbuing will not allow you to generate complete immunities or excessive amounts of damage.
  • You can remove an active imbuement from a slot for 15,000 gold so that it can hold another imbuement.
  • To imbue an item, you need to carry the required astral sources in your inventory. Moreover, you need to have enough gold in your inventory, Gold Pouch, or bank to pay the imbuing fee.
  • A private imbuing shrine for houses can be bought in the Store.
  • You can only imbue unequipped items that are in your backpack.
  • If you try to use an imbuing shrine with an item that cannot be imbued, a message will tell you that this item is not imbuable.
  • The screenshots in the teaser show the imbuing interface in Tibia 11.






Please also note:
With the winter update about 180 already existing items will become imbuable and 63 imbuements will be introduced. Certain types of equipment, such as legs or rings and amulets, or certain effects are not yet available since we plan to enrich and expand the imbuing system in future updates. Moreover, we also plan to incorporate already existing ways to enhance items (enchanting, charging) at some point of time.
